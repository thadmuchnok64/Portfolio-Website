Lumenblade

This was a project for my 3D animation class with professor Karlson at the University of Florida. I was extremely proud of this when I finished it. I remember going in with a completely different aesthetic in mind; initially it was going to be very colorful and take place in a forest during a sunrise. After I rendered the scene for the first time, I accidentally used too little light; however, I ended up being really impressed with what was rendered as it had a dark and unique aesthetic that I had not seen in many animations prior. I am very happy that I made that mistake, as I feel like the end product I ended up with, was better than I imagined; which is saying a lot considering its usually such a challenge for an artist to put their vision completely into the art that they make.

Outline Shader

When working on my game, I was starting to feel that the game needed a graphical style change that fit with the low-poly aesthetic of the world. I thought that adding an outline to all of the meshes would create an interesting look. I am very happy with the end result as one of my friends in the MiDAS program described it as looking like a fairytale illustration.

Enemy Models

Here's some of the models that I made for my current, and most ambitious project to date. I am working on a game where you play as an Angel going down from Heaven into the underworld. The designs are inspired by biblically accurate angels, and holy crusaders. For each enemy, I modeled, UV'd, rigged, and animated them myself. I am trying to model the enemies based around the weapons that the player has. For example, if the player has a shotgun with a wide spread, they would be smart to use it on the enemies with a wider frame such as the crusaders. On the other hand, a weapon that has more precision but less damage would be best at countering the skinnier angels with three rings.
